Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

At Redlands Pest Pros, we strongly believe in and practice Integrated Pest Management (IPM). This is an environmentally sensitive and common sense approach to pest control that relies on a combination of practices to effectively and economically manage pest damage. IPM programs use current, comprehensive information on the life cycles of pests and their interaction with the environment. This information, in combination with available pest control methods, is used to manage pest damage by the most economical means, and with the least possible hazard to people, property, and the environment.

Key Principles of IPM

The core principles of our IPM approach include:

  • Pest Identification: Accurate identification of pests is the first step to understanding their biology and behavior.

  • Monitoring: Regularly checking for pests to determine population levels and assess the success of control measures.

  • Prevention: Implementing measures to prevent pests from becoming a problem, such as sealing entry points and reducing attractants.

  • Control: Using a variety of control methods, starting with the least toxic and moving to more intensive methods only when necessary.

  • Evaluation: Regularly evaluating the effectiveness of the control program and making adjustments as needed.

IPM emphasizes prevention and uses pesticides only when necessary and with careful consideration. This approach is not only more sustainable but also more effective in the long term, reducing the reliance on chemicals and promoting a healthier environment. We strive to stay updated on the latest IPM techniques and best practices to provide you with the most advanced and effective pest control solutions available.
